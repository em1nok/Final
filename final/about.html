ARMY


The name ARMY appeared in the BTS fandom on July 9, 2013

This is one of the most, and perhaps already the most numerous fandom in the world. According to Forbes, according to various estimates, it consists of 40 million to 90 million loyal fans of the South Korean boy band BTS. Armi has created an active online community, which includes about 40 million subscribers to the group's YouTube channel and more than 30 millionfollowing posts on Twitter and on the official Big Hit Instagram account.

ARMY stands out among other fandoms in that they were able to mobilize internationally for the first time in history with a level of organization that has never been seen before. All the armies are driven by the desire to see how seven idols from BTS leave their mark on music, and actively help their favorites in this.ARMY is not just a fan base. They have used their power for social and political action around the world. More accurately, it can be described as a global movement for social justice. According to Esquire, the fandom raised money for rainforest restoration, food for refugees and whale rescue. BTS fans paid for dance lessons for talented children in Rwanda and improved the Internet in rural areas so that schoolchildren could study normally during the pandemic.BTS and ARMY have become the flagships of the new dynamics of fandom: instead of exchanging postcards and gifts, fans collect donations. They transform the world around them day after day in the name of the love of pop music. Armies can do a lot and do a lot in order to be worthy of the love of their idols. The power of fandom is growing year after year, and its influence has long gone beyond k-pop.

This is an appeal to the younger generation, who freely express their emotions and are inspired by the creativity of BTS. Fans calling themselves ARMY treat each member of the band with special respect and affection, and also actively support their creative activities.
